Active This Month — April 2025
Make an Eco-Friendly Gudi
Festival: Gudi Padwa — Maharashtra New Year
Traditionally, a Gudi (a decorated bamboo stick with silk cloth) is hoisted on Gudi Padwa. This year, instead of buying new synthetic cloth, use an old saree or cotton dupatta from home. Make natural dyes from turmeric (yellow), hibiscus flowers (pink/red), and spinach (green). Document how natural pigments differ from synthetic ones — this connects directly to your Science Ch. 4 (Acids, Bases & Salts) and Art & Craft crossover!
Concepts Linked: Acids & Bases, Natural Pigments, Decomposition, Upcycling
Due: 30 April 2025
Classes 6, 7 & 8
Photo + Short Video Required

Newspaper Kite — No Plastic!

Plant a Native Flowering Shrub
On the festival of spring, plant a native shrub like Hibiscus or Jasmine (not an exotic species). Track its growth weekly in a journal. Learn pollination and ecosystems — Science Ch. 12 (Reproduction in Plants).
Concept: Pollination, Ecosystems

Holi
Natural Colour Lab at Home
Skip chemical colours this Holi. Extract pigments from beet (red), turmeric (yellow), spinach (green), and marigold (orange). Compare pH using lemon juice — linking to Ch. 5 (Acids, Bases & Salts). Document each colour's source.
Concept: Acids & Bases, Pigments
+140 pts earned

Buddha Purnima
Create a Silence & Mindfulness Garden
Set up a small corner at home or school with 3 native plants, pebbles, and a water bowl for birds. Link to the concept of interdependence in ecosystems (Ch. 17 — Forests). Document the birds or insects that visit.

June Upcoming
Eid ul-Adha + Monsoon Begins
DIY Rainwater Harvesting Jar
Build a working rainwater harvester from a large plastic bottle, gravel, sand, and charcoal (natural filter layers). Collect and measure rainfall for 2 weeks. Connects to Ch. 18 (Wastewater Story) and the Water Cycle concept.
Concept: Water Cycle, Filtration
+160 pts
July Upcoming
Guru Purnima
Teach a Neighbour to Compost
In the spirit of Guru Purnima (passing on knowledge), teach one neighbour or family member how to compost kitchen waste. Set up a small compost bin together from a plastic container. Link to decomposers — Ch. 17.
Concept: Decomposers, Nutrient Cycle
+140 pts
August Upcoming
Ganesh Chaturthi
Seed-Paper Ganesh Idol That Grows!
Make a small Ganesh idol from seed-embedded handmade paper (newspaper pulp + tomato/marigold seeds). After visarjan in a pot of soil, it dissolves and grows into plants — zero water pollution! Links to soil and decomposition.
Concept: Decomposition, Soil, Germination
+200 pts
September Upcoming
Onam
Pookalam with Wild & Native Flowers Only
Create a traditional Onam flower carpet (Pookalam) using only wild, native flowers found near your home — no bought or exotic flowers. Document the species, their local names, and what insects they attract.
Concept: Native Biodiversity, Pollinators
+130 pts
October Upcoming
Navratri / Dussehra
Upcycle Old Cloth into Dandiya Sticks
Wrap old cloth strips in bright colours around wooden sticks to make Dandiya sticks — zero plastic, zero new material. Learn about fibre types, natural vs synthetic (Ch. 3 — Fibre to Fabric). Also document how much waste you saved.
Concept: Natural Fibres, Upcycling
+130 pts
November Upcoming
Diwali
Hand-Made Clay Diyas with Mustard Oil
Make clay diyas at home from local clay (or mud + straw). Use mustard or groundnut oil as fuel — both biodegradable, no chemical smoke. Compare pollution levels (CO2, particulate matter) from Chinese lights vs clay diyas. Science: Ch. 18.
Concept: Air Pollution, Combustion
+180 pts
December Upcoming
Christmas / Winter Solstice
Orange Peel & Cinnamon Room Freshener
After eating oranges, collect the peels. Dry them, add cloves and cinnamon sticks, and make a natural aromatic sachet or simmer pot. This uses microorganism science (decomposition if peels are left vs. when dried) — Ch. 2 Microorganisms.
Concept: Microorganisms, Decomposition
+120 pts

How Tasks Work
4 simple steps from task to points
1. Pick Your Task
Choose the current month's festival task or an NGO challenge. Read the full description and linked lesson chapter.
2. Do the Task
Complete the hands-on eco-activity at home or in your community. Apply the textbook science concept in real life.
3. Upload Proof
Take a photo and a short 1–2 min video of your completed task. Upload it to the proof page for review.
4. Earn Points
Once verified, points are added to your account and your rank on the leaderboard updates automatically.
